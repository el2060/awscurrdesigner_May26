Section 1

1.  SYNOPSIS AND AIMS

This module aims to provide students with an understanding and comprehensive foundation on the principles of critical care nursing practice and a continuity of basic nursing science from general nursing training. Gastro-intestinal, hepato-biliary, endocrine and nephrology diseases and altered conditions of these major body systems are covered in depth with integration of pathophysiology and applied human anatomy. Interpretation of relevant laboratory and diagnostic studies results are included and advanced nursing skills that are performed commonly in the acute care setting, this module serves to equip critical care practitioners with the necessary knowledge and skills for critical thinking to make informed clinical decisions.

Section 4

4.  TEACHING METHODOLOGY / TEACHING & LEARNING STRATEGIES

Case-Based Learning

In Case-Based Learning, students are typically posed a realistic story or set of circumstances, or complex situation. Students will actively learn to integrate knowledge, skills and attitudes using analytical and critical thinking skills to make decisions or solve issues at various points. They are encouraged to explore assumptions and alternatives, or even future factors, to arrive at different solutions or conclusions.

Section 5

5.  ASSESSMENT STRATEGIES

This is a continuous assessment module and the weightages for the assessments are as follows:

Group Project 50%

The students are required to explore and critically analyse a case scenario. A variety of approaches and delivery methods can be used for the group project presentation. The assessment encompasses 30% group and 20% individual components.

Class Test 1 25%
Class Test 2 25%
Section 6

6.  PLAGIARISM POLICY

The Ngee Ann Polytechnic Plagiarism policy mandates that students must acknowledge the original author when they submit work. Failing to do so will constitute plagiarism.

Students are expected to reference their individual case study report and case presentation slides as per American Psychology Association (APA) format. Students assume full responsibility for the content and integrity of the academic work they submit.
