Embed the Presentation Impact Toolkit as a learning resource
The SoH&IS Presentation Impact Toolkit is a student-facing reference for the techniques the rubric assesses. Embed it directly into your module — it appears in Week 14 (project briefing) and as a downloadable pre-class resource.
Verbal Techniques
What students say
• Clear main message in one sentence
• Strong opening hook (question, statistic, scenario)
• Logical structure (problem → solution → impact)
• Signposting ("Three key points…", "Let's move on")
• Avoid fillers (um, like, basically)
• Active voice, examples & short stories
• Strong closing — takeaway or call to action
Maps to: Verbal Skills
Non-Verbal Techniques
How students show up
• Purposeful gestures matching content
• Open palms — signals confidence & openness
• Avoid fidgeting, pacing, playing with pen
• Facial expression matches message
• Natural smile when appropriate
• Eye contact across the room (not just slides)
• Upright posture, controlled movement, face audience
Maps to: Non-Verbal Skills
Vocal Techniques
How students sound
• Pitch — vary to avoid monotone; high for excitement, low for authority
• Tone — match the message (persuasive, reflective, urgent)
• Volume — louder for key points, softer to draw in
• Pace — don't rush; slow for important ideas
• Strategic pauses before/after key statements
Maps to: Verbal + Non-Verbal
