Suggested drafting considerations
Common themes across uploaded references

Hospital partner discussions, employer feedback, graduate outcomes, and module review inputs suggest increasing emphasis on clinical interpretation, escalation decision-making, and inter-professional communication within critical care nursing contexts.

Suggested learning outcome considerations
  • Consider including an outcome on interpreting AI-assisted monitoring alerts and patient trend data for escalation decisions.
  • Consider strengthening integration across pathophysiology, diagnostics, and clinical risk indicators when justifying nursing interventions.
  • Consider emphasising inter-professional handover and documentation practices in high-acuity care settings.
Suggested content emphases
  • Prioritise commonly encountered ICU scenarios referenced by partner hospitals, including GI bleed, acute pancreatitis, and liver deterioration cases.
  • Introduce early-warning interpretation, risk scoring, and escalation workflow discussions where relevant.
  • Sequence learning from clinical foundations towards integrated case management with alignment to learning outcomes.
Suggested teaching & learning approaches
  • Use blended delivery with concise concept inputs followed by guided case-based application.
  • Incorporate simulation activities using monitoring dashboards, lab trends, and structured clinical huddles.
  • Include formative checkpoints with debrief feedback on reasoning, communication, and escalation decisions.
Suggested assessment considerations
  • Use case-based assessments involving interpretation of monitoring alerts, diagnostics, and intervention prioritisation.
  • Combine individual clinical reasoning with team-based simulation and handover activities where appropriate.
  • Align rubric criteria to decision-making, patient safety, communication clarity, and documentation quality.
